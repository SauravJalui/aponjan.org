20th OCT

Kalparambha, Akal Bodhan, Amantran and Adhivas

‘Bodhon or Akal Bodhon’ is to awaken and welcome Maa Durga as a guest. Maa is then given ‘Amantran’ or an invitation to accept the Navpatrika Puja to be performed on the next day.

  • Puja: 8:30am
  • Pushpanjali: 11:30am
  • Aarti: 12:30am
  • Prasad & Bhog Distribution: 1:00pm
  • Bodhan, Amantran & Adhibash: 7:00pm
  • Sandhya Arati: 7:30pm
  • Cultural Programme: 8:00pm

21st OCT

Navpatrika Puja, Mahasnan, Prana Pratishthan

Navpatrika/Kolabou Puja - Nine plants bathed in holy water worshipped as Nav patrika. ‘Mahasnan’ – Maa is offered a symbolical bath by placing a mirror is to show her reflection. ‘Prana Pratishthan’ – Maa’s murti is consecrated with mantras.

  • Puja: 8:30am
  • Pushpanjali: 11:00am
  • Prasad & Bhog Distribution: 1:00pm
  • Sandhya Arati: 7:00pm
  • Cultural Programme: 8:00pm

22nd OCT

Kumari Puja, Sandhi Puja

Nine girls worshipped as nine forms of Maa called "Kumari Puja". Sandhi Puja, involving the offering of 108 lotuses and lighting of 108 lamps.

  • Maha Ashtami Puja: 8:30am
  • Pushpanjali: 11:00am
  • Maha Ashtami Aarti: 12:10pm
  • Prasad & Bhog Distribution: 1:00pm
  • Sandhi Puja: 4:54pm-5:42pm
  • Sandhya Arati: 7:00pm
  • Cultural Programme: 8:00pm

23rd OCT

Durga Balidan, Navami Homa

Homa (Fire oblation)/Chandi Homa performed with versed from Durga Saptashati.

  • Puja: 8:30am
  • Pushpanjali: 11:10am
  • Prasad & Bhog Distribution: 1:00pm
  • Chandi Paath & Yagya: 1:30pm
  • Sandhya Arati: 7:30pm
  • Cultural Programme: 8:00pm

24th OCT

Durga Visarjan, SindoorUtsav

The last day of this festival, marked by Sindoor Khela (for women) - where in married women applies vermillion to one another. and Kolakuli (for men).

  • Puja: 9:30am
  • Pushpanjali: 11:00am
  • Dodhi Karma & Darpan Bisarjan: 12:00pm
  • Prasad & Bhog Distribution: 12:30pm
  • Sindur Utsav: 1:00pm
  • Immersion: 4:00pm
  • Shantijal & Bijaya Sammelan: 9:00pm

We Bengalis' are scattered everywhere around the world. This particular category of Indian people has always made a name for themselves when it comes to wits and its application.

Another thing that can be associated with us Bengalis' is our celebrations. We enjoy our heart out when it comes to a festive occasion. We intend to maintain this informal contact with all probashi* Bengalis.

We are creating this opportunity for the Bengalis' to come closer and for other people interested in bengali culture to learn more about it. Aponjan.org will provide us a virtual platform to stay in touch with "Bangla Kristi**" throughout the world.

Aponjan is celebrating it’s 15th Durga Puja this year. Come join us in our celebrations.

English translation for Bengali words used:
* Probashi - Bengali living outside West Bengal
**Bangla Kristi - Bengali Culture
